Meet the Team behind Whisker President

This project is developed as a vertical slice for our capstone game design studio course at UCSC.

How we designed this game to fit externally imposed constraints:

Accessibility

  • Limited Vision (Seeing) Your game must be completable even when simulating vision limitations: contrast and color sensitivity.

    Our game does use color distinctions at the moment, however it will also include UI symbols to differentiate such as a dead fish or a bone.

  • Limited Dexterity (Touching) Your game must be completable by a player using just one hand on the controls at a time. You may require multi-button mouse, multi-finger touchscreen gestures, or multi-key keyboard gestures, but you are encouraged to keep the core controls of your game very very simple.

    Our game can be played with just pressing the arrow keys and the trackpad, or using the mouse, which all can be done with one hand.

    • Limited Audition (Hearing) Your game must be completable by a player who cannot hear any sounds from the game (e.g. playing the game with system audio muted). Sonic art is an appreciated aspect of design polish (and it can help reinforce feedback provided by the game's graphics). However, for this inclusive design requirement, auditory feedback cannot required for the player to complete the game.

    Our game is suitable for people with hearing impairments since it can be played without any audio.

  • Limited Energy and Attention (Resting) During a completion run of your game, gameplay should always reach a natural resting point within two minutes of active play without the player needing to take an extra action to pause the game. (The player may still face intense encounters, but they should somehow resolve or get to a situation where it is safe to let go of the controls quickly.)

    The player can let go of the controls at any time during the two minute active play time without any punishments.

Localization

  • Your game must support players two use two different written languages. At least one language must use either a logographic script (e.g. KanjiLinks to an external site.) or a right-to-left script (e.g. ArabicLinks to an external site.). The ability to localize them game to additional written languages should be supported by your localization kit.

    • We have created an asset string table using Unity's localization settings to support the game text in English and simplified Chinese. Each language settings will be set as a different build.

Game Format

  • The game must be entirely completable while the player's device is disconnected from the internet. The total data transfer involved in downloading the game must be less than 100 MiB. The game may target the platform of the team's choice (e.g. desktop, mobile, console, browser, etc.).

    • We have committed to delivering a Unity offline game for desktop platforms with a Windows/Mac build that supports web browsers. The total download size is under 100Mib.

Game Leads


Production Lead: Kirsten Lindblad

Responsible for leading the team towards achieving a scalable goal which includes the game deliverable, using taskflow tools such as Trello to keep track of tasks in order to reach the broader goal. Facilitates communication and discussion while resolving internal disputes.


Design Lead: Michael Law

Responsible for coming up with new and intrinsic design choices that will lead to a better gameplay experience. Documents the shared vision for game for everyone on the team to realize the vision clearly. Consults with tools and engine lead for ensuring design can be integrated into builds and engines.


Engine Lead: Abraham Halim

Responsible for taking charge of the game's engine or platform to build the game and to ensure everyone has enough support to use the engine's capabilities. Finds solutions to problems that occur in the engine and troubleshoots and debugs as needed.


Tools Lead: Samuel Zhang

Responsible for deciding on the main version of engine the team will use, any extensions to support scripting/deployment tasks such as collaborative development or automatic pushing, providing documentation on tool usage, and ensuring all members are following one style guide for written code.


Playtesting Lead: Samip Niraula

Responsible for conducting and facilitating playtests with peers to gather feedback and make necessary changes to game in order to reach better quality demos or final release. Actively researching ways to make the game more testable such as cheat keys or alternative launching mechanisms.
